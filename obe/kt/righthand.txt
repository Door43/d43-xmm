====== right hand ======

===== Definition: =====

The figurative expression "right hand" refers to the place of honor on the right side of a ruler or other important individual.

  * The right hand is also used as a symbol of power, authority, or strength.
  * The Bible describes Jesus as sitting "at the right hand of" God the Father as the head of the body of believers (the Church) and in control as ruler of all creation.
  * A person's right hand was used to show special honor when placed on the head of someone being given a blessing (as when Israel blessed Joseph's son Ephraim).
  * To serve at someone's right hand means to be the one whose service is especially helpful and important to that person.

===== Translation Suggestions: =====

  * If "right hand" does not have the same meaning in the language of translation, then other ways to translate this could include, "power" or "authority" or "place of honor" or "helper" or "is an idiom in the language and has the same meaning, then it can be used to translate this term.
  * When talking about Yahweh, a phrase such as "his right hand and his mighty arm" is a figure of speech that refers to his power and great strength. These two ways of saying the same thing emphasize this point. (See: [[parallelism]])
  * If this would give a wrong meaning or would have no meaning in the language of translation, then a different idiom or term should be used.
  * Depending on the context, other ways to translate "at the right hand of" could include, "to the right of" or "beside him to the right" or "in the place of honor" or "on the right side of."
  * The idiom could also be combined with an explanation such as, "in the place of honor on the right side of."
  * The expression "their right hand is falsehood" could be translated by, "even the most honorable thing about them is corrupted" or "their place of honor is corrupted by deception."
  * In Psalm 105:6, God calls for an accuser to be at the place of honor and strength of a wicked leader sent to punish a rebellious people. This could be translated as, "appoint an accuser to have the place of honor beside that wicked leader" or "appoint an accuser to help that wicked leader punish them" or "punish them even more by sending an accuser to support that wicked leader."
